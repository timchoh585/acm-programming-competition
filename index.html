ACM Presents This Year's 100 and 200 level Programming Competition


Yes you read that right, a programming competition catered to your level!


Compete solo or form a team of at most 3 students where all team members are on the same level. For this programming competition, you may choose to compete solo or with a team of at most 3 students. All team members must be from the same level.

For example, if you are a CS 141 student forming a team then all other team members must also be in 100 level CS courses. No 200 level students are allowed on a 100 level team. The same rule applies for 200 level teams. No 200 level team may have a team member on the 100 level.


100 level teams will only compete against other 100 level teams. 100 Level means: CS 111, CS 141, CS 151.

200 level teams will only compete against other 200 level teams. 200 level means: CS 211, CS 251, CS 261.

You must choose one of the following languages: C, C++, or Java.

Outside resources are allowed such as the Internet and old code
